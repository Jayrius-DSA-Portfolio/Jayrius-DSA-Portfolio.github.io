P5 FLL

In this competition, there was a field with many items, like the Hybrid Car and the television. We had to complete as many missions as possible in two minutes and thirty seconds. We planned the whole run to be split into 4 runs. Run 2 was the smallest and run 4 had the most to execute. Our robot had many extensions that could sometimes do more than 1 mission. Our robot used color sensors to catch some of the isolated lines and used proportional, double, and single-line tracing to navigate through the field. During the runs, I and my team managed to change the extensions quickly to ensure that the least amount of time was used to ensure that the robot had more time to carry out the run. Due to the time constraint, our robot only managed to do runs 1 and 2. We had to use one precision token that allowed us to transfer our robot to the other side of the field and skip run 3. This competition taught me how to systematically plan my missions and how time was precious in a run.
